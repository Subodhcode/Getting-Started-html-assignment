Dr. A.P.J Abdul Kalam

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, India's 11th President, was a revered scientist, aerospace engineer, and an inspirational figure. Born in 1931, he played a pivotal role in India's missile development and space programs, earning him the moniker "Missile Man of India." Dr. Kalam's dedication, humility, and passion for education made him a beloved leader. His presidency (2002-2007) emphasized the importance of innovation and education. Tragically, he passed away in 2015 while delivering a lecture, leaving a profound legacy of inspiration and service to the nation. Dr. Kalam's life serves as a beacon of hope and determination for generations to come.

Sadly, Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong. His death was mourned by the entire nation, and he left behind a lasting legacy as a role model,
scientist, and leader who dedicated his life to the service of his country and its people. Dr. Kalam's life story continues to inspire generations of Indians to dream big, work hard, and contribute to the progress of their nation.
